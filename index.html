The Ancient Process

Born in the Sacred Desert Plains of Rajasthan

Sambhar Lake Salt has been harvested in India for over a thousand years. Naturally alkaline and sun-cured through the ancient process of Suryatapa.

Capillary Rise

Salt pans form as groundwater salts rise by capillary action; when water evaporates, minerals remain, making the soil alkaline.

1
2

Brine Channeling

Brine from Sambhar Lake is added to pans, followed by subsoil brine, concentrating the mineral-rich waters under the Rajasthani sun.

Dunaliella salina

The rare pink microalgae Dunaliella salina thrives in these brine beds, giving the salt its distinctive pink hue and adding iodine naturally.

3
4

9–12 Month Sun-Drying

Salt crystals are sun-dried for 9 to 12 months, locking in minerals. The warmth of the Indian sun and the harmony of Ayurveda shape each grain.

Pure Harvest

Crystals are hand-collected from Sambhar Sarovar and packed in their purest, most natural form — no refining, no bleaching, no additives.

5
6

Eco-Friendly Packaging

Salt is cleaned and packed in recyclable, eco-friendly packaging — this traditional lavanam brings mineral-rich purity and ancestral wellness to everyday meals.
